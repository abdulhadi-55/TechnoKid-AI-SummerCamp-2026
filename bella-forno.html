00 Why We Fire the Oven

Why Make Pizza the Slow Way?

Have you ever walked past a pizzeria and been stopped by the smell of dough hitting a hot stone?

Real pizza starts long before the oven — with flour, water, salt, and time. It takes patience, a hot fire, and years of practice to get right.

Doing it the slow way matters because it helps us:

  • Bring out real flavor from simple ingredients
  • Support local farms and suppliers
  • Keep an old craft alive
  • Bring people together around one table
ReflectEvery great pizza begins with a simple question — what if we let the dough take its time?
01 What Is Real Pizza?

What Makes Pizza "Real"?

Real pizza is a balance of four things — dough, sauce, cheese, and fire. It officially became a protected tradition in 1984, when Naples set rules for what counts as true Neapolitan pizza.

A proper pie is different from a frozen one in a few key ways:

  • The dough is hand-stretched, never rolled with a pin
  • There's no sogginess, since the crust is baked fast at very high heat
  • The center stays slightly soft, called "al dente" in Italian pizza tradition
NotePizza isn't just cheese and bread — it carries the flour, the water, and even the air of the kitchen it's made in.
02 History of Pizza

History of Pizza

People have topped flatbread for centuries. Here's how it became the dish we know today.

1889
The Margherita pizza is created in Naples, topped in the colors of the Italian flag.
1905
Lombardi's opens as America's first pizzeria, in New York City.
1943
The first deep-dish pizza is served in Chicago.
1958
Pizza Hut opens, making pizza a fast, casual meal nationwide.
1984
Naples establishes official rules for true Neapolitan pizza.
2014
Bella Forno opens its doors, built around one wood-fired oven.
TakeawayPizza's history shows that a simple idea, done well, can travel the whole world.

04 Life of a Pizzaiolo

A Day in the Life of Our Pizzaiolo

Becoming a pizzaiolo takes years of practice, and a shift behind the oven looks nothing like a quiet kitchen.

  • They mix dough the night before every shift
  • They stretch by hand, never with a rolling pin
  • They work just 18 inches from a 900°F flame
  • They turn each pizza every 20 seconds in the oven
  • They read the crust by color, not by a timer
Did you knowA skilled pizzaiolo can shape a ball of dough into a stretched round in under 30 seconds.

08 Steps of Making a Pizza

From Order to Table

  1. Order taken — your pizza and toppings are chosen
  2. Dough stretched — shaped by hand into a round base
  3. Toppings added — sauce, cheese, and your chosen extras
  4. Fired in the oven — baked at 900°F in under two minutes
  5. Sliced and served — straight from the peel to your table
